Tetris Legends

Willis Gibson

Username(s) / Gamertag(s)

Blue Scuti

Accomplishments

Willis Gibso, also known as Blue Scuti, is best known for "beating" Tetris on the NES on December 21, 2023. He triggered a killscreen at level 157, causing the game to crash. In addition to this feat, he also placed third in the Classic Tetris World Championship (CTWC) in October 2023. Currently, Willis Gibson is considered one of the best Tetris player in the United States

Jonas Neubauer

Username(s) / Gamertag(s)

nubbinsgoody, Bunbun

Accomplishments

Jonas Neubauer was a seasoned Tetris player having won seven times at the Classic Tetris World Championship prior to the hypertapping era. In addition to having won seven times at the Classic Tetris World Championship, he was also a previous world record holder for a high-score of 1,245,200 points. He is also attributed to being one of the greatest Tetris players of all time.

  • Wikipedia

Alex Thach

Username(s) / Gamertag(s)

Alex T

Accomplishments

Alex Thach, age 16, was the first person to score over 10 million points and was the first person to reach level 235. He is also the world champion for the 2024 Classic Tetris World Championship. He is also considered the 4th person to ever beat Tetris, triggering the killscreen like Willis Gibson did when he beat the game.

Sources:

  • Wikipedia

Justin Yu

Username(s) / Gamertag(s)

Fractal161

Accomplishments

Justin Yu is best know for his victory in the 2023 Classic Tetris World Championship. He is also considered the second person to beat Tetris. Additionally, he is the first person to get the earliest game crash. Justin Yu's playstyle is the "rolling" playstyle, a playstyle that allows for positioning game pieces more quickly.

Sources:

  • Wikipedia

Michael Artiaga

Username(s) / Gamertag(s)

dogplayingtetris, Dog

Accomplishments

Michael Artiaga won both the 2020 and 2021 Classic Tetris World Championship, winning the 2020 championship earned him the title of "Youngest Tetris World Champion" at the age of 13. Michael Artiaga was also the youngest person to complete a "maxout" on November 2019.
